RAG Embedding Benchmark

zembed-1: We tested ZeroEntropy's new embedding model

UM
Umida Muratbekova

ZeroEntropy released zembed-1 a few days ago. It's a 4 billion parameter embedding model distilled from their zerank-2 reranker. We ran it through our evaluation suite to see how it performs on real retrieval tasks.

TL;DR

zembed-1 achieved 0.946 NDCG@10 on web search, 94% recall on Arabic, and 0.854 on finance. It's the highest-scoring model on MSMARCO in our leaderboard. Performance drops on scientific and entity-heavy content.

What We Found

Best web search score we've recorded

On MSMARCO—the standard web search benchmark and the closest proxy to real RAG workloads—zembed-1 hit 0.946 NDCG@10. That's the highest score across all 16 models in our leaderboard.

MSMARCO tests short queries against web-scale content, which is what most RAG systems actually look like in production. A 0.946 means zembed-1 consistently puts the right documents at the top. In RAG, that directly affects how good the generated answers are.

Wins most head-to-head matchups

We ran pairwise comparisons against all 15 other models. The chart shows wins (green) and losses (red) for each matchup, sorted by margin. At the top: zembed-1 beat Gemini 004 in 48 out of 55 comparisons. Against Jina V3 and Cohere V3, it won 40 and lost around 14.

At the bottom of the chart: the closest matchups. Against OpenAI Large, it's 27-27. Against Voyage 4, 32-28. These are essentially coin flips, which puts zembed-1 in the same tier as established models.

Strong multilingual without giving up English

On the Arabic dataset (ARCD), zembed-1 reached 94% recall in top-10 results with 0.858 NDCG@10. This is notable because multilingual models typically sacrifice English performance for cross-lingual coverage. zembed-1 doesn't. It holds top-tier English web search scores while also delivering strong Arabic retrieval.

How We Tested

  • Each model embedded the same corpus and queries
  • Retrieved top-10 results per query, calculated NDCG@10 and Recall@10
  • Elo ratings from pairwise comparisons using GPT-5 as judge (930 query pairs)
  • 5 datasets with human-annotated relevance: MSMARCO (web), FiQA (finance), SciFact (science), DBPedia (entities), ARCD (Arabic)

Conclusion

The benchmark suggests zembed-1 works well when:

  • your RAG pipeline handles general web content or search-style queries
  • you serve multilingual users and want one embedding model instead of several
  • finance or business documents make up a large part of your corpus
