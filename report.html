What is zembed-1?

zembed-1 is a 4 billion parameter multilingual embedding model from ZeroEntropy. It was distilled from their zerank-2 reranker model, which likely explains some of its strong performance in retrieval tasks.

The model outputs 2048-dimensional embeddings by default but supports flexible dimension reduction down to 40 dimensions, as well as quantization from 32-bit floats down to binary. This makes it practical for production deployments where you need to balance quality against storage and compute costs.

Key specs: 4B parameters, 2048 dimensions (flexible to 40), distilled from zerank-2 reranker, supports document/query input types, available via EU API endpoint.

How zembed-1 Performed

zembed-1 achieved a 58.9% win rate across 930 pairwise comparisons, resulting in the highest Elo score of 1587.65. But the aggregate numbers hide some interesting patterns in the per-dataset and per-model results.

The standout performance is on MSMARCO (0.9465 NDCG@10) and FiQA (0.8545), which aligns with ZeroEntropy's claim that zembed-1 excels on finance, healthcare, and legal domains. The model also performs well on the Arabic dataset (ARCD), suggesting good multilingual capabilities.

Note: Business Reports and PG datasets don't have human-annotated relevance judgments, so NDCG metrics aren't calculated for those. They're evaluated via LLM judge only.

Methodology

How the Evaluation Works

  • LLM-as-Judge: GPT-5 compares top-5 search results from each model for the same query
  • Position Debiasing: Results are randomly swapped (A/B positions) to prevent position bias
  • Sample Size: 10 queries per model pair per dataset (930 total judgments for zembed-1)
  • Elo Calculation: Standard Elo with K=32, initialized at 1500, 10 iterative passes
  • Datasets: FiQA, MSMARCO, SciFact, DBPedia, ARCD (with NDCG); Business Reports, PG (LLM judge only)

Takeaways

zembed-1 is a strong new entrant in the embedding model space. Its #1 Elo ranking comes from consistent performance across the board rather than dominance at the top—it beats most models convincingly while staying competitive with the best.

If you're building RAG applications in finance, healthcare, or legal domains, zembed-1 is worth evaluating. The flexible dimension reduction (2048 down to 40) also makes it practical for production use cases where you need to trade off quality for efficiency.

The main caveat is we don't yet have proper ground truth for long document retrieval. The Business Reports dataset uses synthetic relevance labels, so take those results with a grain of salt.
